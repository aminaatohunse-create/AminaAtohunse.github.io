About Me

I am Aminat Atohunse, a First Class Geography graduate from the University of Ibadan and the Best Graduating Student of the 2023/2024 academic session. My interests lie at the intersection of climate and environmental sustainability, remote sensing, GIS, and Earth observation.

I am passionate about using geospatial technologies and data-driven approaches to understand environmental change, support sustainable development, and inform evidence-based decision-making. My experience spans GIS mapping, remote sensing analysis, spatial data interpretation, environmental research, and quantitative data analysis using tools such as ArcGIS, QGIS, SPSS, and Python.

Through academic research, professional experience, and independent learning, I have worked on projects involving land-use change, urban expansion, environmental management, waste management, and spatial analysis. I am continually developing my skills in programming, data science, web development, and geospatial analytics to contribute to innovative solutions for climate and sustainability challenges.

This portfolio showcases selected projects, maps, reports, research activities, and technical skills that reflect my commitment to leveraging Earth observation and geospatial intelligence for environmental sustainability and societal impact.
